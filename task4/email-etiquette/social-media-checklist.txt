The "Dos": Master Your Presence
Do Optimize for Social Search: Many people now use TikTok or Instagram like Google. Use natural keywords in your captions and video scripts so your content actually shows up when people search for topics you care about.

Do Fact-Check Before Sharing: With AI-generated misinformation being common, your credibility is your currency. Take ten seconds to verify a "breaking news" story before hitting repost.

Do Embrace "Low-Fi" Authenticity: The era of the "perfectly curated grid" is over. Users (and algorithms) now prefer "Behind the Scenes" content, "Day in the Life" vlogs, and raw, human storytelling over highly polished, plastic-looking posts.

Do Use AI as a "Sparring Partner": Use AI tools to brainstorm captions or edit video transitions, but don't let it write your entire personality. People can spot "bot-speak" from a mile away.


The "Don'ts": Avoid the Cringe and the Risks

Don’t Over-Automate: Automated "thanks for following" DMs or AI-generated bot comments are the fastest way to lose followers. Real engagement requires a human touch.

Don’t Fall for "Engagement Bait": Avoid the "Comment 'YES' if you agree!" or "Tag a friend who..." tropes. Modern algorithms are increasingly penalizing these "low-effort" tactics.

Don’t Tag Everyone in Everything: Over-tagging or using 30+ hashtags is seen as "spammy." Stick to 3–5 highly relevant tags that actually describe your content.

Don’t Ghost Your Community: If someone takes the time to leave a thoughtful comment, reply to it. In 2026, a small, deeply engaged community is worth more than 100k "ghost" followers.
